kimray

Launch New Session

The Kimray valve application, built for the New Mexico Junior College, is a live 3D Immersive Maintenance Demonstration built on top of Virtual World Framework to demonstrate the capabilities of Remote Subject Matter Experts interacting with a student using a shared real time lesson workspace. This application utilizes WebRTC to deliver real time video and voice over IP without any browser plugins.


bzflag

Launch New Session

The bzflag application is the Virtual World Framework's version of the classic arcade game. Use the WASD keys to navigate the scene and the Enter key to fire a laser. Use the chat feature to communicate and taunt your opponents, but most of all look out for opposing tanks!

Who's Using VWF

Advanced Distributed Learning


Advanced Distributed Learning (ADL) has utilized VWF to create a sandbox application that allows the user to create a world directly in the browser. Additionally, the ADL server allows for persistent worlds that can be shared with others over time. Check out their Sandbox application above.


Analytical Graphics, Inc.


Analytical Graphics, Inc. (AGI) provides COTS software to national security and space professionals for integrated analyses and visualization of land, sea, air, and space assets. AGI has integrated Cesium, their WebGL based virtual globe, with the framework.


Cole Engineering Services, Inc.


Cole Engineering Services, Inc. (CESI) has explored the integration of VWF with JBUS and the EDGE platform.


J7


The Joint Staff J7 supports the joint warfighter through Joint Force Development in order to advance the operational effectiveness of the current and future joint force, and is utilizing VWF to create a virtual joint operations center to support JS J7's traditional joint collective training responsibilities, as well as improve learning and execution across the broader training spectrum - to include enhancing individual joint training and infusing unit home-station training with joint and coalition context.

Lively


Lively Kernel, a new approach to web programming, provides a complete platform for web applications, including dynamic graphics, network access, and development tools. Lively and VWF have teamed up to explore integration between the two architectures. Current exploration and further information can be found on GitHub.


Lockheed Martin


Lockheed Martin is internally developing projects with the Virtual World Framework for maintenance and task training in domains such as Defense, Marine, and Energy.


New Mexico Junior College


New Mexico Junior College (NMJC) is taking advantage of the remote collaborate nature of the framework, creating maintenance training applications that support real time interactions with instructors. Check out their Kimray Valve application above.


Z Shift


Z Shift is developing VWF applications for a virtual command center in correlation with the US Joint Force Development (J7).
